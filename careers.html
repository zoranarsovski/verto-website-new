Craft

We invest in your craft.

Most agencies promise growth. We put a number on it.

  • Personal training budget

    Scales with seniority. Use it on the conferences, courses, or coaches that matter for your level — not just a shared L&D pot.

  • Coursera for everyone

    Plus any other course you can make a case for. The bar is low — a paragraph on why it helps you or a client is usually enough.

  • 130–160 training hours a year

    That's 11–14% of working time — a line item in the plan, not a wish-list.

  • Coaching for senior team members

    External coaches, paid for, scheduled during working hours. Not a reward — an assumption.

  • Power skills training, twice a year

    The stuff that compounds: feedback, presenting, running a difficult meeting, managing up.

  • Transparent career paths

    The criteria to move to the next level are written down and shared — so "what do I need to do to get promoted?" has an actual answer.

  • Real reviews, not ratings

    Annual goal setting, a mid-year, and an end-of-year — with concrete feedback, not a number on a spreadsheet.

Growth here isn't a slide in the onboarding deck. It's a line item.

What we believe

Our values.

Six lines we wrote down, then tried to keep honest. If they sound like the people you'd want to work with, you'll like it here.

01 — Explore

We Explore

Curiosity before certainty. We'd rather ship a rough experiment and learn than argue the perfect framework for a quarter.

02 — Create Value

We Create Value

Pipeline and revenue are the scoreboard. If the work doesn't move one of them, we ask why before we ask for the budget.

03 — Shine

We Shine

Own your craft. Bring your best, visibly — in the work, in the meeting, in the pull request. Quiet excellence still counts.

04 — Get It Done

We Get It Done

Ship beats perfect. When the path is obvious, move. When it isn't, pick one and commit. Rework is cheap; drift is expensive.

05 — Share

We Share

Defaults to open. What you learn belongs to the team before it belongs to you — the playbook, the failure, the Loom, the one useful line.

06 — Care

We Care

About teammates, about clients, about the work — on the hard days especially. "Yes, and" beats "no, but" almost every time.

FAQ

Questions people ask.

What's the interview process like?

Usually a first chat with the hiring manager, a craft-focused task or work sample, and a final round with the team you'd work with. Two to three weeks end-to-end. No trick questions.

What does hybrid actually mean?

Three days in the office, two flexible. We value the in-person time — especially in your first 3–6 months when you're ramping up.

Which offices hire for which teams?

Most roles can be based in any of our three Bulgarian offices. A few roles are Sofia-only by default — we'll note that clearly in the job description.

Do you hire juniors?

Yes, regularly. We hire for curiosity and trajectory as much as current skills — especially in SEO, Paid, and Analytics.

What languages do I need?

Fluent English for client work. Bulgarian is useful internally but not required for most roles.

Do I need to be in Bulgaria?

For most roles, yes. Some remote flexibility exists, but we expect you to be in-country and in the office for your first 3–6 months.
