How we work together

Our values.

Here's what we'd ask of you, and what you can ask of us. If any of these would annoy you to live with day-to-day, we're probably not your people — and that's fine.

01 — Explore

We Explore

Curiosity before certainty. We'd rather ship a rough experiment and learn than argue the perfect framework for a quarter.

02 — Create Value

We Create Value

Pipeline and revenue are the scoreboard. If the work doesn't move one of them, we ask why before we ask for the budget.

03 — Shine

We Shine

Own your work. Bring your best, visibly — in the meeting, in the deliverable, in the pull request. Quiet excellence still counts.

04 — Get It Done

We Get It Done

Ship beats perfect. When the path is obvious, move. When it isn't, pick one and commit. Rework is cheap; drift is expensive.

05 — Share

We Share

Defaults to open. What you learn belongs to the team before it belongs to you — the playbook, the failure, the Loom, the one useful line.

06 — Care

We Care

About teammates, about clients, about the work — on the hard days especially. "Yes, and" beats "no, but" almost every time.

FAQ

Questions people ask.

What's the interview process like?

Usually a first chat with the hiring manager, a practical task or work sample, and a final round with 2–3 people from the team you'd work with. Two to three weeks end-to-end. No trick questions.

What about salary?

Each role lists its salary range and contract type on TalentHR. We'll discuss your expectations openly in the first call — bring yours. On top of base, the team's profit-based bonus has averaged 2× base salary across the last three years.

Is the work sample paid?

It's time-boxed to ~2–3 hours and unpaid at that scope — the kind of work we need to see to make a decision. If we ask for more than that, we pay your day rate. We don't believe in 3-day "test projects".

What does hybrid actually mean?

Three days in the office, two flexible. We value the in-person time — especially in your first 3–6 months when you're ramping up.

Which offices hire for which teams?

Most roles can be based in any of our three Bulgarian offices. A few roles are Sofia-only by default — we'll note that clearly in the job description.

Do you hire juniors?

Yes, regularly. We hire for curiosity and trajectory as much as current skills — especially in SEO, Paid, and Analytics.

What languages do I need?

Fluent English for client work. Bulgarian is useful internally but not required for most roles.

Do I need to be in Bulgaria?

For most roles, yes. Some remote flexibility exists, but we expect you to be in-country and in the office for your first 3–6 months.

Will I be on US hours?

No — not by default. Most client calls land 3–6pm Sofia. If something runs past 7pm it's flagged and the next morning is yours. The Boston outpost exists so nobody here has to live on US time.

Do I have to relocate to Sofia?

Only if the role is marked Sofia-only. Most roles are open across Sofia, Ruse, and Blagoevgrad — pick the base that makes your commute sane. Same comp, same work, same team.

Are AI agents replacing roles at Verto?

Honestly: agents are replacing tasks, not people. The manual 60% of a junior's week — audience builds, ad-copy variants, GA4 reports — is what we're automating. The result is that juniors here take on mid-level scope within a year, and nobody's job is the copy-paste part. If anything, we're hiring more people, not fewer.
