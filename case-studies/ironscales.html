Section 02 · The problem

The problem.

What the dashboards said

Click-through rates healthy

Lead volume trending up

Spend-to-conversion ratio stable

What the pipeline review said

CRM conversion data didn't reconcile

Bid algorithms training on form-fills

Pipeline not moving proportionally

The campaign metrics looked good. Click-through rates were healthy. Lead volume was up. The dashboards told a consistent story.

The pipeline review told a different one.

When IRONSCALES looked at the conversion event data Salesforce was actually receiving from paid media, it didn't reconcile with what the ad platforms reported as "conversions." The bid algorithms — Google Ads, LinkedIn — were optimizing against form-fill data that didn't translate cleanly to opportunities. The team was spending more, getting more reported leads, and not seeing proportional pipeline.

This is the gap most B2B paid media programs hit at scale. The problem isn't the campaign. It's the data layer beneath it. Without an offline conversion feedback loop tied to CRM-defined deal stages, the ad platforms can't learn what a real buyer looks like. They learn what a form-filler looks like — and form-fillers include students, vendors, competitors, internal team members, and the occasional real prospect.

IRONSCALES needed the bid algorithms to see deal outcomes, not lead-gen events. That meant rebuilding the data infrastructure underneath the paid program before any campaign work could move the pipeline number.

Section 03 · The work

What we built.

Three components, deployed in twelve weeks. Each is unremarkable on its own. The system is what produced the results.

Component 01

Server-side measurement foundation.

Server-side GTM container deployed. Event taxonomy redesigned for B2B funnel logic, not e-commerce defaults. GA4 cleaned and rebuilt around real conversion events. Data piped to BigQuery for ownership and zero sampling.

sGTM GA4 BigQuery

Component 02

HubSpot offline conversion integration.

Lead-to-CRM stitching verified end-to-end. HubSpot deal-stage events — MQL, SQL, Opportunity, Closed-Won — pushed to Google Ads via offline conversion uploads. Same event stream piped to LinkedIn Conversions API. Microsoft Advertising added for harvest-channel coverage.

HubSpot Google Ads OC LinkedIn CAPI

Component 03

Value-based bidding and audience refinement.

Conversion values assigned per CRM stage and per ICP score. The bid algorithm started bidding harder on accounts that close, not contacts that convert. ICP audiences rebuilt against closed-won lookalikes — what a buyer actually looks like, not what a high-volume form-filler looks like.

What it meant for the team.

The conversation with finance shifted. Paid media stopped being a discretionary cost line and became a defensible source of pipeline. The marketing team could attribute opportunity revenue back to specific campaigns and channels with confidence. The data quality was high enough that quarterly pipeline reviews became conversations, not arguments.

Twelve months in, the program is still compounding. Every closed-won deal feeds back into the seed audience for LinkedIn AI Targeting. Every new opportunity refines the bid algorithm. The system gets sharper over time — not because the campaigns get better, but because the data layer makes the platforms smarter.
