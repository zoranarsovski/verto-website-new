One content layer

Built for both surfaces

Buyers used to type a query into Google and click a result. Some still do. But increasingly, the answer is delivered before they reach a result page — by ChatGPT, by Perplexity, by Google AI Overviews. The buyer never visits your site. They get the answer, and either you were cited as the source or you weren't.

Two surfaces. Two optimization regimes.

  • SEO Built for ranking. Technical foundation, content depth, internal linking, link authority, site speed. The discipline is mature, the mechanics are well-understood, and the work compounds.
  • AEO Built for being cited. Structured content, schema markup, source authority, content "chunks" the LLM can extract, factual specificity, and topical breadth. The discipline is forming. The companies that establish authority now will own the AI search results for the next decade.

Most agencies do the first. Few do the second well. The third — running them as one engine, where technical SEO investments compound AEO authority and vice versa — is what the Verto SEO & AEO program is built around.

Crawlability audit. Core Web Vitals. Schema markup that both Google and LLMs read. JavaScript rendering that doesn't hide content from crawlers. Page speed at the edge. The boring infrastructure that determines whether anything else you do matters.

ICP-defined query mapping. Not "high-volume keywords" — high-fit queries, scored by where they sit in the buying journey. BOF and transactional queries first (fastest path to pipeline influence). MOF queries second (consideration content). TOF queries last (problem-aware authority content).

03 production

04 AEO layer

Content structured in extractable chunks. Schema markup for Articles, FAQs, How-Tos. Source authority built through citations from authoritative domains. 05 compounding

Authority compounds when content forms clusters around buying-journey themes. We map every published asset to the cluster it serves and the conversion goal it drives. The internal link architecture turns isolated articles into a topical authority network.

Who this fits

Who SEO and AEO fits.

Best fit

  • B2B SaaS or B2B technology with at least 18 months runway to compound (organic is a long-cycle channel)
  • Existing site with reasonable technical foundation (or willingness to fix it)
  • Subject-matter experts on the team who can be involved in content review
  • Category with at least moderate search demand and a forming AI-search citation pattern

Not the right fit yet

  • Sub-12-month runway — organic doesn't deliver in time
  • Brand-new category with no search demand and no AI citation history — start with paid awareness
  • Inability to involve SMEs in content review — AEO content needs accuracy beyond what generalist writers produce

FAQ

Questions B2B marketing leaders ask before signing.

What's the difference between SEO and AEO?
SEO optimizes content to rank in traditional search results (Google, Bing). AEO — Answer Engine Optimization — optimizes content to be cited as a source by AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews). Same content layer, different optimization requirements. Most agencies do the first. We do both.
How long until SEO shows results?
Technical foundation and BOF-query content can show ranking movement in 60–90 days. Topical authority compounding takes 6–12 months. Most engagements see meaningful organic pipeline contribution by month 9.
Do you do link building?
We do digital PR and partnership-driven link building tied to topical authority objectives. We don't do paid link buying, link networks, or guest-post-spam tactics. The work is slower; it lasts.
How do you measure AEO performance?
We track citation presence across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AIO for a defined set of target queries. Audit cadence is monthly. Reporting includes citation rate, citation context, and competitor citation share.
Will AI-generated content hurt our SEO?
AI-generated content alone, with no human review, is bad. AI-generated content with subject-matter expert review, brand voice editing, and accuracy verification is fine — and is how the major B2B publishers now operate at scale. Our hybrid model is built on this principle.
Do you write all the content yourselves?
We can. We can also work with your in-house team on a co-production model, or with your existing content agency on a strategy-and-quality-control engagement. Most clients pick a hybrid based on their internal capacity.
Can you guarantee rankings?
No. Anyone who guarantees rankings is either lying or using tactics that will get the site penalized. We agree on leading indicators (publishing velocity, technical health, citation growth) and lagging outcomes (organic pipeline contribution) — and we report on both.
